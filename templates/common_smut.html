Disease Name:

Common Smut
Ustilago maydis


Symptoms

Swollen, gray or white lumps (called galls) on corn ears, leaves, stalks, or tassels.

Galls turn black as they get older and release powdery spores.

Infected plants may look strange or grow poorly.


Cause

Caused by the fungus Ustilago maydis. It lives in the soil and crop trash and spreads through wind and rain.


Prevention and Control

Resistant Varieties: Use corn types that resist smut.

Crop Rotation: Avoid planting corn in the same field every year.

Remove Galls: Take out and destroy infected parts before they burst.

Healthy Plants: Keep soil healthy and avoid too much nitrogen fertilizer.
